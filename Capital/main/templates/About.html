We look for the following when we're conducting our analysis and assessment of current potential investing markets and firms

Market Capitalization

The key aspect we analyze is the present market capitalization and position of the firm in issue. We look for opportunities for growth in this industry and decide whether this firm has the potential to be a leader or the resources and commitment to be at the forefront.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Another important aspect is that we look for strengths and flaws in the company's structure, management, and organization to see if it has the dedication and drive to meet the needs of investors. The next critical criterion is to evaluate the management's strengths and limitations, as well as to see if the organization's motivations align with those of our investors.

Growth Potential

The company's organization and management are next examined in the context of possible expansion, i.e., does it have the organization and desire to adapt and grow? We also consider the company's resources and goals when determining prospective development.

Exit Prospects

We assess the exit opportunities for potential investors, particularly through the public securities market, strategic purchasers, or potential national and international investors or buyers.

Control

We analyze both the majority and minority interests to ensure they correspond with those of our potential investors, since it is critical to understand who is in charge and what views are heard.

Cash Flow

It's critical for the business to have a positive or virtually positive cash flow. Profitability levels are also examined.

Rate of Return

The last criterion is to evaluate that the potential internal rate of return should correspond with the risk of the investor's portfolio of investments.
